The footage was mundane, yet it felt like I was peeking into a world that was frozen in time. The camera panned across the school's courtyard, showing a group of friends – Alex, Emily, Jake, and Sarah – sitting on a bench, chatting and giggling. Everything seemed normal, but there was an underlying sense of unease, like the calm before a storm.

The camera captured a conversation between Alex and Emily, discussing an urban legend about a cursed tape that, when played, would summon an evil entity. They laughed it off, but the atmosphere had shifted. The friends began to act strangely, as if they were being manipulated by an unseen force. I found myself wondering if the legend was more than just a myth.

## Part 3: The Revelation

They were attempting to play the cursed tape, despite their initial skepticism. As they inserted the tape into a old recorder, the camera began to malfunction, and the static returned. But this time, it was different. The static coalesced into a figure, standing just behind the group. The friends froze, paralyzed with fear. The figure began to whisper, its voice like a gentle breeze, but the words were unmistakable: "You should not have played the tape." The camera cut to black, and the TV screen went dark. I was left with more questions than answers. What happened to the group of friends? Was the urban legend true? And what was the purpose of the anonymous letter?
